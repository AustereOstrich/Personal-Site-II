More About Me

A few years ago, you might have found me sitting in my apartment flipping back and forth between two screens on my laptop; one showing the MCAT prep course I knew I should be studying for, and another stacked with videos and text editors as I ran through a course on Python development that I'd stumbled across in my procrastination.

Fast forward a little, and you'd find me in a cramped, windowless conference room listening to my boss ask me what my goals were for my career at the bank where I was working. It was a tough question, but my answer was simple - maybe IT was a better fit for me than mortgage serviving.

The next few years found me working in IT and constantly wondering why we didn't have better tools, more automated processes, more functional software. So I started writing some for myself. I got into a master's program for software engineering. Now I write code all day, and I love every second of it (even the moments when I have no idea why said code refuses to work).
